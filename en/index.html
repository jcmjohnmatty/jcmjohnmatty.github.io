Jellyfish Simulation

This was the final project that was coded in the Introduction to Computer Graphics that I took my Sophomore year. One other person and myself did all of the work. I was primarily in charge of project management, research, the coding of the jellyfish's body, and texturing. My partner was primarily in charge of presentation preparation as well as the coding of the tentacles as well as other project organization.

For the cap of the jellyfish, it's relaxed shape is modeled as half of an ellipsoid, and the vertices used to model it form a spring-mass system. The thrust of the jellyfish is modeled as a modified sinusoidal function with some additional pre and post processing done as well to ensure the correct orientation of the jellyfish and that the appropriate constraints are held on the shape of the jellyfish.

The tentacles were modeled as a particle system with a configurable amount of connected particles. The tentacles were attached to each radial slice of the cap. When the cap pulsates, a series of equations updates the position of each tentacle.

Textures were implemented using OpenGL's texturing mechanism.

This project was also chosen as one of the six best final projects out of the class the semester that I took it.

GPGMK: A GPU Powered 3D Geometric Modeling Kernel

This is a cross platform, GPU powered, 3D, geometric modeling kernel that I have developed. Geometric modeling kernels play an important role in modern CAD systems. They are the core of modern CAD systems, providing geometric modeling algorithms, and base classes to store and implement constructive solid geometry objects. Without them, modern CAD applications most likely would not exist in their current state.

Many current systems, such as AutoDesk and SolidWorks, depend on specific platforms, such as Windows. Their geometric modeling algorithms also lack GPU support, which can seriously inhibit performance.

By implementing these algorithms integrated with GPU support using cross platform technologies, we hope to improve the performance of current geometric modeling kernels, and thus reduce the need to buy top line CPUs currently required for top CAD performance. We also hope to reduce dependence on specific platforms, thus increasing portability, and usefulness, of open source geometric modeling technologies.

Floor Outlet Cover

This is a floor outlet cover I designed by myself. While designing it, I paid special attention to making sure that people walked over it, they would not trip over the covers's top. B of this, the outlet covers's top and the screw heads inserted into it are both level with the floor surface. Also, All of the holes for screws are a standard size, so it is very easy to move it somewhere else. I also took into consideration the wiring that would be under the floor outlet cover. This is because I had to ensure that the floor outlet cover would not destroy any wiring beneath it. Finally, All of the holes, as well as other parts of the floor outlet cover, all have some amount of a tolerance to them, so it's easy to insert screws and put together.

In order to fully test my design, I also 3D printed multiple versions of it along the way, testing to make sure that the desired specifications were met.
